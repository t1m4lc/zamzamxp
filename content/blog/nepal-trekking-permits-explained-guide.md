## Understanding Nepal Trekking Permits

Trekking in Nepal requires various permits depending on your destination. This guide explains all the permits you need, costs, where to get them, and important 2023+ regulation changes including the **mandatory guide requirement**.

---

| Trek | Permits Required | Total Cost (2026) |
|------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Everest Base Camp** | Sagarmatha NP + TIMS | ~$60-70 |
| **Annapurna Circuit** | ACAP + TIMS | ~$50-60 |
| **Annapurna Sanctuary** | ACAP + TIMS | ~$50-60 |

## Types of Permits

### 1. TIMS Card (Trekking Information Management System)

**What it is:** A registration system to track trekkers for safety purposes.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost** | NPR 2,000 (~$15) for individuals, NPR 1,000 (~$7.50) via agency |
| **Required for** | Most trekking regions in Nepal |
| **Validity** | Duration of your trek |
| **Photo required** | Yes, 2 passport-sized photos |

**Where to get it:**
- Nepal Tourism Board (NTB) office in Kathmandu (Bhrikuti Mandap)
- TAAN office in Kathmandu
- Pokhara Tourism Office
- Through your trekking agency (easiest)

---

### 2. ACAP (Annapurna Conservation Area Project)

**What it is:** Entry permit for the Annapurna Conservation Area, protecting the region's environment and communities.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost** | NPR 3,000 (~$23) for foreigners, NPR 200 for SAARC nationals |
| **Required for** | Annapurna Circuit, ABC, Ghorepani, Mardi Himal, Khopra |
| **Validity** | Single entry to the conservation area |
| **Photo required** | Yes, 2 passport-sized photos |

**Coverage includes:**
- Annapurna Circuit (entire route)
- Annapurna Base Camp (Sanctuary)
- Ghorepani Poon Hill
- Mardi Himal
- Upper Mustang (in addition to RAP)
- Nar Phu Valley

---

### 3. Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit

**What it is:** Entry permit for the Everest region (Sagarmatha = Nepali name for Everest).

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost** | NPR 3,000 (~$23) for foreigners |
| **Required for** | Everest Base Camp, Gokyo Lakes, Three Passes |
| **Validity** | Single entry |
| **Photo required** | Yes, 2 passport-sized photos |

**Where to get it:**
- Kathmandu: Department of National Parks and Wildlife Conservation
- Lukla: Entry checkpoint (emergency only, better to get in advance)
- Monjo: Park entrance checkpoint

---

### 4. Langtang National Park Entry Permit

**What it is:** Entry permit for Langtang region treks.

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Cost** | NPR 3,000 (~$23) for foreigners |
| **Required for** | Langtang Valley, Gosaikunda, Helambu |
| **Validity** | Single entry |
| **Photo required** | Yes, 2 passport-sized photos |

---

### 5. Restricted Area Permits (RAP)

**What it is:** Special permits for controlled/restricted regions requiring organized group travel.

| Region | Cost | Minimum Group |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **Manaslu** | $100 (Sep-Nov), $75 (other months) | 2 persons |
| **Upper Mustang** | $500 (first 10 days) + $50/day after | 2 persons |
| **Upper Dolpo** | $500 (first 10 days) + $50/day after | 2 persons |
| **Nar Phu Valley** | $100 (Sep-Nov), $75 (other months) | 2 persons |

**Important:** RAPs can only be obtained through registered trekking agencies.

## 2023 Mandatory Guide Regulation

### ⚠️ Important Change

Since April 2023, **solo trekking without a guide is prohibited** in Nepal. All foreign trekkers must hire:
- A licensed trekking guide, OR
- A porter-guide (combined role)

### Why This Changed

1. **Safety:** Better emergency response for trekkers
2. **Local economy:** Ensures local employment
3. **Regulation:** Better tracking of trekkers in remote areas

### What This Means for You

- Budget for guide costs (~$25-35/day)
- Book through registered agencies
- Guides handle permit applications (easier for you)
- Solo "independent" trekking is no longer legal

## Where to Get Permits in Kathmandu

### Nepal Tourism Board (NTB)
- **Address:** Bhrikuti Mandap, Kathmandu
- **Hours:** Sun-Fri 10am-5pm (closed Sat)
- **Documents needed:** Passport, 2 photos, payment
- **Issues:** TIMS, information on permits

### TAAN Office (Trekking Agencies' Association of Nepal)
- **Address:** Maligaon, Kathmandu
- **Hours:** Sun-Fri 10am-4pm
- **Issues:** TIMS cards

### Department of Immigration (for RAPs)
- **Address:** New Baneshwor, Kathmandu
- **Note:** RAPs processed through agencies only

## Permit Process: Step-by-Step

### If Using a Trekking Agency (Recommended)

1. **Provide documents:** Passport copy, passport photos
2. **Agency handles:** All permit applications
3. **Receive permits:** Before trek departure
4. **Cost:** Included in package or small service fee

### If Arranging Independently (with required guide)

1. **Visit NTB office** for TIMS card
2. **Visit relevant park office** for conservation/national park permit
3. **Bring:** Passport, 4-6 photos, cash (NPR)
4. **Allow:** 1-2 hours for processing

## Checkpoints During Your Trek

You'll encounter permit checkpoints throughout your trek:

| Trek | Major Checkpoints |
|------|-------------------|
| **EBC** | Monjo, Namche (park entrance) |
| **Annapurna Circuit** | Bhulbhule, Chame, Thorong Phedi, Kagbeni |
| **ABC** | Chomrong, MBC |
| **Langtang** | Dhunche |

**Tip:** Keep permits accessible in a waterproof pouch. You'll show them multiple times.

## Tips for Smooth Permit Processing

1. **Bring extra passport photos** (6-8 recommended)
2. **Carry passport copies** (leave original safely stored)
3. **Get permits in Kathmandu** before heading to trailhead
4. **Book through agency** - they handle everything
5. **Keep permits dry** - use waterproof pouch
6. **Don't lose permits** - fines and complications if lost
