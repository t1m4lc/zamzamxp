## Safety Benefits

### Altitude Sickness Recognition

Local guides are trained to recognize **altitude sickness symptoms** before they become dangerous.

| Guide Advantage | Value |
|-----------------|-------|
| **Symptom recognition** | Spot early signs you might miss |
| **Pace management** | Ensure proper acclimatization |
| **Emergency protocols** | Know evacuation procedures |
| **Local health posts** | Know locations and capabilities |
| **Satellite communication** | Carry phones for emergencies |

### Trail Navigation

| Challenge | Guide Solution |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Trail junctions** | Know correct paths without signs |
| **Weather changes** | Adjust routes for safety |
| **Seasonal variations** | Know which trails are passable |
| **Alternative routes** | Avoid landslides or closures |
| **Night navigation** | Experience in emergency situations |

### Emergency Response

| Emergency | Guide Action |
|-----------|--------------|

| **Weather** | Shelter options, route changes |

## What Makes a Good Guide

### Essential Qualifications

| Qualification | Why It Matters |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Government license** | Verified training, accountability |
| **First aid certification** | Emergency response capability |
| **Language skills** | Effective communication |
| **Trail experience** | Specific route knowledge |
| **References** | Proven track record |

### Qualities to Look For

| Quality | Importance |
|---------|------------|
| **Patience** | Different fitness levels, many questions |
| **Flexibility** | Adapting to your interests |
| **Knowledge** | Deep understanding of culture, nature |
| **Personality** | You'll spend 2+ weeks together |
| **Safety focus** | Prioritizes wellbeing over schedule |

### Questions to Ask

Before booking, ask potential guides:

1. How many times have you completed this trek?
2. What's your first aid training?
3. How do you handle altitude sickness?
4. What equipment do you carry?
5. Can you share references from previous clients?
