## Scenery & Landscapes

### Annapurna Circuit: Maximum Diversity

The Annapurna Circuit is famous for its incredible landscape variety. In a single trek, you'll experience:

- **Subtropical zone** (800-2,000m): Rice paddies, banana trees, waterfalls
- **Temperate forest** (2,000-3,000m): Oak, rhododendron forests
- **Alpine zone** (3,000-4,000m): Pine forests, high meadows
- **High desert** (4,000m+): Mustang-style arid landscapes
- **Glacial zone** (5,000m+): Snow, ice, massive peaks

**Best views:** Annapurna I-IV, Dhaulagiri, Machhapuchhare (Fishtail), Manaslu

### Everest Base Camp: Pure Himalayan Drama

EBC focuses on the world's highest peaks and glacial landscapes:

- **Khumbu Valley**: Dramatic glacial valleys
- **Sherpa villages**: Pine forests and Buddhist culture
- **High alpine**: Barren, rocky terrain above treeline
- **Glacial moraine**: Walking on the Khumbu Glacier itself

**Best views:** Mount Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, Ama Dablam, Pumori

## Physical Difficulty

### Annapurna Circuit

- **Biggest challenge:** Thorong La Pass (5,416m) - single hard day
- **Daily walking:** 5-7 hours average
- **Terrain:** Varied - stone paths, suspension bridges, some road sections
- **Altitude gain:** Gradual, good acclimatization profile

### Everest Base Camp

- **Biggest challenge:** Sustained high altitude for multiple days
- **Daily walking:** 5-7 hours average
- **Terrain:** Rocky trails, steep descents, glacier moraine
- **Altitude gain:** More time spent above 4,000m

### üèÜ Winner: Tie - Both require good fitness and altitude preparation

## Cultural Experience

### Annapurna Circuit: Multi-Cultural Journey

You'll pass through territories of multiple ethnic groups:

- **Gurung villages** (lower sections)
- **Manangi people** (Manang valley)
- **Thakali culture** (Mustang area)
- **Buddhist monasteries** throughout
- **Hindu temples** at Muktinath

### Everest Base Camp: Sherpa Heartland

Deep immersion in Sherpa Buddhist culture:

- **Namche Bazaar** - the "Gateway to Everest"
- **Tengboche Monastery** - most famous in Nepal
- **Sherpa hospitality** in family-run lodges
- **Everest climbing history** and memorials

### üèÜ Winner: Annapurna (variety) | Everest (depth)

## Logistics & Access

### Annapurna Circuit

- **Getting there:** 6-8 hour drive from Kathmandu to Besisahar/Jagat
- **Reliability:** Road access is weather-dependent but rarely cancelled
- **Flexibility:** Multiple exit points if needed
- **Cost:** Lower overall (no flight required)

### Everest Base Camp

- **Getting there:** 35-minute flight Kathmandu ‚Üí Lukla
- **Reliability:** Lukla flights often delayed/cancelled due to weather
- **Flexibility:** Only one way in and out
- **Cost:** Higher (flight ~$350-400 round trip)

### üèÜ Winner: Annapurna (reliability and cost)

---

## Crowds & Teahouse Availability

### Annapurna Circuit

- **Peak season:** Moderate crowds, teahouses available
- **Route options:** Multiple variations possible
- **Booking:** Reservations recommended but not always essential

### Everest Base Camp

- **Peak season:** Very crowded, especially October-November
- **Single route:** Everyone follows the same path
- **Booking:** Advance reservations highly recommended

### üèÜ Winner: Annapurna (fewer crowds)
