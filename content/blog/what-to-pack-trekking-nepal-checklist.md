## The Essential Nepal Trekking Packing List

Packing for a Nepal trek requires balancing preparation with weight. You'll need protection from extreme weather while keeping your backpack manageable. This comprehensive checklist covers everything from Everest Base Camp to Annapurna Circuit and Langtang Valley treks.

### Golden Rule: Pack Light, Pack Right

- **Daypack target:** 6-8 kg (what you carry daily)
- **Duffel for porter:** 10-15 kg maximum
- **Total gear:** Should fit in a 60-70L duffel + 30-40L daypack

## Clothing: The Layering System

### Base Layers (Next to Skin)

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Moisture-wicking underwear | 3-4 | Quick-dry synthetic or merino wool |
| Sports bras (women) | 2-3 | High support, moisture-wicking |
| Thermal base layer top | 2 | Merino wool recommended |
| Thermal base layer bottom | 1-2 | For sleeping and cold days |
| Hiking socks | 4-5 pairs | Merino wool blend, medium cushion |
| Liner socks | 2 pairs | Optional, prevents blisters |

### Mid Layers (Insulation)

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Fleece jacket | 1 | 200-300 weight |
| Down/synthetic jacket | 1 | Packable, 600+ fill for high altitude |
| Softshell jacket | 1 | Optional, for windy conditions |
| Trekking pants | 2 | Zip-off convertible recommended |
| Fleece pants | 1 | For evenings and cold mornings |

### Outer Layers (Protection)

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Waterproof rain jacket | 1 | Gore-Tex or equivalent, hooded |
| Waterproof rain pants | 1 | Lightweight, packable |
| Windbreaker | 1 | Optional if you have rain jacket |

### Head, Hands & Feet

| Item | Quantity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Sun hat | 1 | Wide brim for lower altitudes |
| Warm beanie | 1 | Fleece or wool |
| Buff/neck gaiter | 1-2 | Versatile: sun, dust, cold protection |
| Liner gloves | 1 pair | Touch-screen compatible |
| Insulated gloves | 1 pair | For high passes |
| Trekking boots | 1 pair | Broken-in, ankle support, waterproof |
| Camp shoes/sandals | 1 pair | Flip-flops or Crocs for teahouses |

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Sunscreen | SPF 50+, high altitude UV is intense |
| Lip balm | SPF 30+, prevents cracking |
| Hand sanitizer | Essential, water not always available |

## First Aid & Health

### Medical Kit Essentials

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Diamox (Acetazolamide) | Altitude sickness prevention (prescription) |
| Ibuprofen/Paracetamol | Pain and fever |
| Imodium | Diarrhea |
| Oral rehydration salts | Dehydration |
| Antihistamine | Allergies |
| Throat lozenges | Dry mountain air |
| Blister plasters | Compeed or similar |
| Medical tape | Various uses |
| Antiseptic wipes | Wound cleaning |
| Bandages | Assorted sizes |

### Health Notes

⚠️ **Diamox**: Consult your doctor before trekking. Many trekkers take 125mg twice daily starting 1 day before ascending above 3,000m.

## Electronics & Documents

### Electronics

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Phone | Download offline maps (Maps.me) |
| Power bank | 20,000mAh recommended |
| Charging cables | USB-C and Lightning |
| Universal adapter | Nepal uses Type C, D, M plugs |
| Camera | Optional, phones work fine |
| E-reader | Optional for downtime |

### Documents

| Item | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Passport | Valid 6+ months, with visa |
| Passport copies | 2-3 photocopies |
| Passport photos | 4-6 for permits |
| Travel insurance | Must cover high altitude trekking |
| Emergency contacts | Written copy |
| Cash (USD & NPR) | ATMs only in major towns |
| Credit card | Backup, not widely accepted |

## What NOT to Bring

❌ **Cotton clothing** - Stays wet, causes hypothermia  
❌ **Heavy books** - Use e-reader instead  
❌ **Laptop** - Unnecessary weight  
❌ **Expensive jewelry** - Leave at home  
❌ **Too many "just in case" items** - You can buy basics in Kathmandu  
❌ **Large toiletries** - Decant into travel bottles  

---

## Where to Buy Gear in Kathmandu

If you're missing items, Thamel has hundreds of trekking shops selling:
- Quality knock-off brands (50-70% of Western prices)
- Genuine brands at similar prices to home
- Rental gear (sleeping bags, down jackets)

**Tip:** Check gear quality carefully. Real North Face vs. "North Fake" varies widely.

## Packing by Trek Type

### Everest Base Camp / Annapurna Circuit (High Altitude)

- **Sleeping bag:** -15°C rated essential
- **Down jacket:** Must-have above 4,000m
- **Insulated gloves:** For Thorong La/Kala Patthar

### Langtang Valley (Moderate Altitude)

- **Sleeping bag:** -10°C rated sufficient
- **Lighter layers:** Less extreme conditions
- **Rain gear:** More important (lower = wetter)

## Final Checklist Before Departure

✅ Trekking boots broken in (50+ km walked)  
✅ All medications labeled and packed  
✅ Travel insurance covers altitude trekking  
✅ Passport photos ready for permits  
✅ Downloaded offline maps  
✅ Emergency contacts shared with family
