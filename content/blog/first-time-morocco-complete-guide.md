## Welcome to Morocco: What to Expect

**Morocco** is a land of contrasts – ancient medinas meet modern cities, Saharan dunes transition to Atlantic beaches, and Berber traditions blend with Arab influences. For first-time visitors, it can be overwhelming but incredibly rewarding.

### Quick Facts

| Info | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Capital** | Rabat |
| **Language** | Arabic, Berber, French (widely spoken) |
| **Currency** | Moroccan Dirham (MAD) |
| **Time Zone** | GMT+1 (no daylight saving) |
| **Visa** | 90-day visa-free for most Western countries |
| **Best Time** | March-May, September-November |

## Top Regions for First-Time Visitors

### 1. Marrakech & Surroundings

The classic introduction to Morocco:
- **Djemaa el-Fna:** Iconic square with food stalls and entertainment
- **Medina:** Labyrinthine old town with souks
- **Atlas Mountains:** Day trips to Berber villages
- **Essaouira:** Coastal escape (2.5 hours away)

**Best for:** Culture, shopping, food lovers

### 2. Essaouira

Our favorite coastal gem:
- **Surfing:** Year-round waves for all levels
- **Medina:** UNESCO World Heritage, relaxed vibe
- **Seafood:** Fresh catches daily
- **Wind sports:** Kitesurfing and windsurfing paradise

**Best for:** Beach lovers, surfers, relaxation

### 3. Fes

Morocco's cultural heart:
- **Fes el-Bali:** World's largest car-free urban area
- **Tanneries:** Famous leather production
- **Crafts:** Authentic artisan workshops
- **Food:** Refined Moroccan cuisine

**Best for:** History buffs, authentic experience seekers

### 4. Sahara Desert

The ultimate Moroccan adventure:
- **Merzouga:** Gateway to Erg Chebbi dunes
- **Camel trekking:** Sunrise/sunset rides
- **Desert camps:** Sleep under the stars
- **Berber culture:** Traditional music and hospitality

**Best for:** Adventure seekers, photographers

## Cultural Tips & Etiquette

### Do's ✅

| Tip | Why |
|-----|-----|
| **Learn basic Arabic/French** | "Shukran" (thank you), "La" (no), "Salam" (hello) |
| **Dress modestly** | Cover shoulders and knees, especially in medinas |
| **Bargain in souks** | Start at 50% of asking price |
| **Accept mint tea** | Refusing is considered rude |
| **Ask before photographing people** | Especially women and in rural areas |
| **Use your right hand** | For eating, greeting, and giving |

### Don'ts ❌

| Avoid | Reason |
|-------|--------|
| **Public displays of affection** | Not culturally accepted |
| **Drinking alcohol publicly** | Only in licensed establishments |
| **Entering mosques** | Non-Muslims cannot enter (except Hassan II) |
| **Discussing politics/religion** | Sensitive topics |
| **Taking photos of military/police** | Strictly prohibited |

## Safety in Morocco

Morocco is generally **safe for tourists**, but awareness helps:

### Common Concerns

| Issue | How to Handle |
|-------|---------------|
| **Touts/Guides** | Politely decline with "La, shukran" |
| **Scams** | Agree on prices beforehand, use official taxis |
| **Getting lost in medina** | Stay calm, ask shop owners for directions |
| **Pickpockets** | Keep valuables secure, especially in crowds |

### For Solo Female Travelers

- Dress conservatively to minimize attention
- Ignore catcalls – don't engage
- Stay in well-reviewed riads/hotels
- Consider guided tours for desert trips
- Morocco is safe, but extra awareness helps

## Money & Budget

### Daily Budget Estimates

| Style | Budget (USD/day) |
|-------|------------------|
| **Backpacker** | $30-50 |
| **Mid-range** | $60-100 |
| **Comfortable** | $120-200 |

### Money Tips

1. **Cash is king** – ATMs available, but carry cash for souks
2. **Bargain everywhere** – Except in restaurants with posted prices
3. **Tip modestly** – 10-15% in restaurants, small tips for services
4. **Change money** – Banks offer better rates than hotels

## Getting Around

### Between Cities

| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| **CTM/Supratours buses** | Comfortable, reliable, affordable |
| **Trains (ONCF)** | Marrakech-Casablanca-Fes corridor |
| **Grand taxis** | Shared rides between cities |
| **Domestic flights** | Long distances (Marrakech-Fes) |

### Within Cities

| Method | Notes |
|--------|-------|
| **Walking** | Best for medinas |
| **Petit taxis** | Metered, within city limits |
| **Uber/Careem** | Available in major cities |

## Food & Drink

### Must-Try Dishes

| Dish | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Tagine** | Slow-cooked stew in clay pot |
| **Couscous** | Traditional Friday dish |
| **Pastilla** | Sweet-savory pigeon/chicken pie |
| **Harira** | Hearty tomato-lentil soup |
| **Mint tea** | "Moroccan whiskey" – sweet mint tea |
| **Msemen** | Flaky, pan-fried flatbread |

### Vegetarians

Morocco can be challenging for vegetarians – many dishes have hidden meat stock. Request "bidoun l'ham" (without meat) and opt for:
- Vegetable tagines
- Zaalouk (eggplant salad)
- Salads and bread
- Eggs (available everywhere)
