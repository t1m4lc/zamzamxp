## Essential Information About the Everest Base Camp Trek

The **Everest Base Camp (EBC) trek** is the most iconic trekking route in the world, taking you to the foot of Mount Everest (8,849m), the highest peak on Earth. This legendary journey through the Khumbu region offers breathtaking Himalayan panoramas, rich Sherpa culture, and the ultimate sense of achievement.

### Key Figures of the Everest Base Camp Trek

- **Duration:** 12-14 days (standard itinerary)
- **Distance:** ~130 km round trip
- **Maximum altitude:** 5,364 m (Everest Base Camp) / 5,545 m (Kala Patthar viewpoint)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate to Challenging
- **Best period:** Spring (March-May) and Autumn (September-November)
- **Trek type:** Lodge-based (teahouses)
- **Guide mandatory:** ⚠️ Since 2023, a certified guide is MANDATORY
- **Required permits:** Sagarmatha National Park Entry Permit + TIMS Card

## Physical Preparation

Start training **at least 3 months** before your trek:

### Month 1-2: Build Endurance
- Cardio: 30-45 min, 4-5 times per week (running, cycling, swimming)
- Hiking with a loaded backpack (start with 5kg, increase to 10kg)
- Stair climbing or step machine

### Month 3: Trek-Specific Training
- Long hikes (6-8 hours) on weekends
- Altitude simulation if available
- Core and leg strengthening exercises

## Altitude Sickness Prevention

The key to a successful EBC trek is proper acclimatization:

1. **Follow the "climb high, sleep low" principle**
2. **Stay hydrated:** 3-4 liters of water daily
3. **Avoid alcohol and sleeping pills**
4. **Recognize AMS symptoms:** headache, nausea, dizziness, fatigue
5. **Descend immediately** if symptoms worsen

## Why Trek with a Local Guide?

Since 2023, trekking with a certified guide is mandatory in Nepal. Here's why it's beneficial:

- **Safety:** Guides know the terrain and can recognize altitude sickness
- **Cultural insight:** Learn about Sherpa traditions and Buddhist practices
- **Logistics:** Permit handling, accommodation booking, route optimization
- **Support local economy:** Fair wages for local communities
